Yala National Park

Yala National Park is Sri Lanka's most visited and second-largest national park, renowned for having the highest density of leopards in the world. This vast wilderness spans 979 square kilometers of diverse ecosystems including forests, grasslands, and lagoons.

Experience thrilling wildlife encounters with elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, and over 215 bird species. Yala offers the best chance to spot the elusive Sri Lankan leopard in its natural habitat.

Park Highlights

  • Highest leopard density in the world
  • Large elephant herds (300+)
  • Sloth bears and water buffalo
  • 215+ bird species including painted storks and peacocks
  • Coastal sand dunes and lagoons
  • Ancient Buddhist monastery ruins

Hidden Gem

Lunugamwehera National Park

Lunugamwehera is a pristine wilderness area bordering Yala National Park, offering a more peaceful and exclusive safari experience. This 235 square kilometer park is centered around the magnificent Lunugamwehera Reservoir.

Less crowded than Yala, Lunugamwehera provides intimate wildlife encounters in untouched natural habitats. The park serves as an important elephant corridor and sanctuary for diverse wildlife.

Park Highlights

  • Untouched wilderness, fewer crowds
  • Large elephant populations
  • Beautiful Lunugamwehera Reservoir
  • Sambar deer, spotted deer, wild boar
  • Leopard sightings possible
  • Excellent bird watching opportunities

Bird Paradise

Bundala National Park

Bundala National Park is a wetland paradise and birdwatcher's dream, designated as a Ramsar Wetland of International Importance. This 62 square kilometer coastal park features lagoons, marshes, sand dunes, and thorn forests.

Famous for its massive flocks of migratory water birds, Bundala is home to thousands of flamingos during migration season and hosts 197 bird species, making it one of Sri Lanka's most important bird sanctuaries.

Park Highlights

  • Greater flamingo sanctuary (thousands visit)
  • 197+ bird species, 58 migratory
  • Five coastal lagoons
  • Mugger crocodiles and estuarine crocodiles
  • Small elephant herds
  • Rich reptile diversity including sea turtles
